Steak

A perfectly seared steak seasoned with salt, pepper, and herbs, cooked to juicy perfection and served with garlic butter.

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye or sirloin steaks (about 1 inch thick)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme sprigs (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp steak seasoning or paprika (optional)

Steps

  1. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This helps them sear better.
  2. Season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and steak seasoning if using. Let rest for 10–15 minutes at room temperature.
  3. Heat a skillet or grill pan over high heat. Add olive oil and let it get hot until shimmering.
  4. Place the steaks in the pan and sear for 3–4 minutes on each side for medium-rare (adjust time for desired doneness).
  5. Add butter, crushed garlic, and herbs to the pan. Spoon the melted butter over the steaks for added flavor during the last minute of cooking.
  6. Remove the steaks from the pan and let them rest for 5–10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
  7. Slice and serve with your favorite sides, drizzling any remaining pan butter over the top.
